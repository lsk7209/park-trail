# Sample: A Gentle Trail Is a Decision, Not a Label

A trail becomes gentle only when the numbers, surface, route shape and day conditions fit the people walking it. The label "easy" can help with discovery, but it cannot decide for a family with a tired child, a visitor managing knee pain or a group arriving after a long drive.

The Gradient Trail voice slows that decision down. It asks which signal matters most, what evidence supports it and what official condition could override it before the visit.

| Signal | Better question | Planning use |
|---|---|---|
| Distance | Is the full outing comfortable after stops? | Prevents short-route overconfidence. |
| Grade | Is the hard part hidden in one short pitch? | Finds effort the label may hide. |
| Surface | Is the path paved, gravel, sand, boardwalk or mixed? | Connects terrain to real visitors. |
| Current condition | What alert could change the plan today? | Keeps official sources in control. |
